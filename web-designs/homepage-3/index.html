I like to make things on computers and with electonics. I don't like to do ordinary activities because it's not unique, breaking free from a monophonic society and documenting some creative and different projects on this website.

How I got into computing:

Before programming, I enjoyed creating mechanical machines using Lego Technic. My most notable inventions were the Mechanical Useless Box and several Pneumatic powered engines powered by (not so) compressed air. Later, I developed an intrest for creating projects using Scratch and experimenting with it's limitations. Keep in mind I was less than 10 years old when I was doing this. When I realised how marvelous coding was, I advanced to Python and Javascript where I developed my first major projects. During this, I developed a liking for retro/8-bit computers, which is why I included a photograph of the National Museum of Computing in Bletchley Park (Aug 2024), a planed journey back is required in 2025/6.
